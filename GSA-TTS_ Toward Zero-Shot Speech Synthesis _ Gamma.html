    Abstract
    We present the gradual style adaptor TTS (GSA-TTS) with a novel style encoder that gradually encodes speaking styles from an acoustic reference for zero-shot speech synthesis. GSA first captures the local style of each semantic sound unit. Then the local styles are combined by self-attention to obtain a global style. This semantic and hierarchical encoding strategy pro- vides a robust style representation for an acoustic model. We test GSA-TTS on unseen speakers and obtain promising re- sults regarding naturalness, speaker similarity, and intelligibil- ity. Additionally, we explore the potential of GSA in terms of interpretability and controllability which stems from its hierar- chical structure.
