GSA-TTS: Toward Zero-Shot Speech Synthesis based on Gradual Style Adaptor

 

 

Anonymous submission to InterSpeech 2024

ABSTRACT

We present the gradual style adaptor TTS (GSA-TTS) with a novel style encoder that gradually encodes speaking styles from an acoustic reference for zero-shot speech synthesis. GSA first captures the local style of each semantic sound unit. Then the local styles are combined by self-attention to obtain a global style. This semantic and hierarchical encoding strategy provides a robust style representation for an acoustic model. We test GSA-TTS on unseen speakers and obtain promising results regarding naturalness, speaker similarity, and intelligibility. Additionally, we explore the potential of GSA in terms of interpretability and controllability which stems from its hierarchical structure.
